Cities’ Public Parks Comparison

Analysis
Python
OSM
Published

19/04/2026

Overview

In this project, I compare the availability, size and accessibility of public parks across three similar cities in central Israel, located near each other. This type of analysis can support urban planning and quality-of-life assessments.

The cities are:

  • Hod Hasharon (pop. ~63,000)
  • Kfar Sava (pop. ~101,000)
  • Raanana (pop. ~75,000)

The cities are considered as “dormitory cities”, located in the southern Sharon region in Israel and have a high socio-economic level. All three cities have central parks as well as smaller parks spread throughout residential areas.

The comparison includes the number of parks, their total area, mean area, total area per 1,000 residents, and the relative coverage of 500-meters buffer zones.

Spatial Data

  • OpenStreetMap: leisure: [park, garden]
Note

OpenStreetMap data may be incomplete or inconsistent, so results should be interpreted as approximate.

Workflow

  • Looping through the cities’ boundaries and extracting the parks info for each city.
  • Park information is structured as Dictionary - Key: City’s name, Value: Parks GeoDataFrame
  • CRS transformation to Israeli TM Grid (EPSG: 2039)
  • Spatial analysis
  • Simple analysis with static and interactive visualization

CRS:

For this analysis I’ll use Israel 1993 / Israeli TM Grid (EPSG: 2039)

Cities boundaries:

Extract cities boundaries 
# Create list of osm_id:
osm_id_list = cities_df['osm_id'].to_list()

# Create a gdf of the OSM features:
geoms_gdf = ox.geocode_to_gdf(osm_id_list, by_osmid=True)

# Attach the geometry column to the original cities_df:
cities_df['geometry'] = geoms_gdf['geometry'].values

# Convert cities_df to cities_gdf:
cities_gdf = gpd.GeoDataFrame(cities_df, geometry='geometry', crs='EPSG:4326')

Cities parks:

Extract parks info 
# Creating dictionary of cities' parks(key = city name, value = parks gdf)
# For each gdf - clean data and calculate parks' areas

parks = {}
tags = {'leisure': ['park', 'garden']}

for row in cities_gdf.itertuples():
    
    # Extracting OSM data and convert CRS:
    parks[row.name] = ox.features_from_polygon(
        row.geometry, 
        tags=tags
        ).to_crs(epsg=2039)


    # Clearing out non Polygon/MultiPolygon features:
    parks[row.name] = parks[row.name][parks[row.name].geometry.geom_type.isin(
        ["Polygon", "MultiPolygon"])]

    # Clearing out unnecessary columns:
    parks[row.name] = parks[row.name][["geometry", "name"]]

    # Calculating area for each park:
    parks[row.name] = parks[row.name].assign(
        area_m2=lambda df: df.geometry.area
    )

# Changing the cities CRS to epsg: 2039
cities_gdf = cities_gdf.to_crs(epsg=2039)

Analysis

Basic Statistic

Calculate parks statistics 
# Total numbers of parks per city:
cities_gdf["parks_no"] = cities_gdf["name"].apply(
    lambda x: len(parks[x]))

# Total parks' area per city:
cities_gdf["total_parks_area_dunam"] = cities_gdf["name"].apply(
    lambda x: parks[x]["area_m2"].sum())/1000

# Average parks' area per city:
cities_gdf["mean_parks_area_dunam"] = cities_gdf["name"].apply(
    lambda x: parks[x]["area_m2"].mean())/1000

# Total parks' area per 1,000 population:
cities_gdf["park_area_per_1k_pop"] = round(cities_gdf["total_parks_area_dunam"] / cities_gdf["population_k"], 2)
Note

1 Dunam = \(1,000 m^2\)

Parks Coverage

While park availability provides a general comparison, it does not capture how easily residents can actually reach these parks. Accessibility is estimated by creating 500-meters buffer around parks (representing approximate walking distance) and calculating their coverage relative to the total city area.

Note

Land-use and residential distribution are not considered in this analysis.

The results should be interpreted as a relative measure of spatial proximity rather than actual walking accessibility.

Calculating parks buffer coverage area 
# Create buffer 500 m around the parks
cities_gdf['parks_buffer'] = cities_gdf['name'].apply(lambda city_name: parks[city_name].buffer(500).union_all())

# Define as geometry type column and set CRS
cities_gdf.set_geometry("parks_buffer", inplace=True)
cities_gdf["parks_buffer"] = cities_gdf["parks_buffer"].set_crs(epsg=2039)

# Calculating cities area
cities_gdf["city_area"] = cities_gdf.set_geometry("geometry").area/1000

# Calculating un-buffered area
cities_gdf["unbuffered_area"] = (
    cities_gdf["geometry"].difference(cities_gdf["parks_buffer"]).area / 1000
)


# Calculating un-buffered percentage
cities_gdf["unbuffered_perc"] = cities_gdf["unbuffered_area"] / cities_gdf["city_area"] * 100

Note: Cities with large peripheral parks may appear more accessible in buffer-based analysis, even when those parks are less usable for daily access.

Key Findings

  • Hod Hasharon has fewer parks overall, both by number and total area
  • Raanana has the largest number of parks, but their average area is the smallest, nevertheless, it maintains the highest park area per capita.
  • Kfar Sava has the largest average parks area
  • Both Kfar Sava and Raanana have similar parks’ accessibility coverage characteristics which covers almost the whole cities area, while Hod Hasharon has a significant portion of its area outside the 500-meter accessibility. (Note: Most of the areas in south-west Hod Hasharon are open fields and industrial)

Conclusion

As we can see from Figure 1 the three cities have different public parks characteristics.

Hod Hasharon has significantly fewer parks (63) compared to the other two cities. Although its parks are slightly larger on average (7.09 dunam), this does not compensate for the lower total park area (446.54 dunam) and park area per capita (7.09 Dunam per 1,000 population).

Kfar Sava has fewer parks (109) than Raanana, but they are larger on average (7.63 dunam). In contrast, Raanana has more parks (179) with smaller average size (4.44 dunam), resulting in the highest park area per capita among the three cities.
(Raanana has 10.59 Dunam per 1,000 population, while Kfar Sava has 8.23.)

The higher parks area per capita in Raanana may reflect lower population density or different urban planning priorities.

In Figure 2 we can see the parks potential accessibility of 500 meters zone buffer around the parks and its relatively area to the cities total areas. Here as well, Hod Hasharon has significantly lower ratio (61.1%) compared to Kfar Sava (83.6%) and Raanana (86.7%)

The lower ratio in Hod Hasharon is caused mainly in the city’s residential spread which is concentrated in its eastern areas.
